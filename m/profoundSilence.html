Profound Silence

‘It was a possible future?’ Ved was thoughtless.

‘Yes.’ Yug Replied.

Ved had no clue what Yug was saying. Everything went over his head. Though things were hard to believe, what he had faced and seen was real. With Yug giving him an explanation, Ved had no alternative option but to accept what he was saying.

However, he couldn’t bear the thought that his friends could go against him, and even try to kill him. Furthermore, even if Peter and Hussain would force, Ved thought, Steve will never stand for something like that. He was not only his subordinate but a close friend as well.

He had chosen to listen to him earlier, as he says, but he resisted ‘Steve is like my brother. Others are good friends as well. I don’t think they can even think of doing something like this.’

‘It might be hard for you to believe, but everyone wears a mask on his face. Each and every individual ultimately lives for himself Ved. You shall realize this soon as we progress ahead, nobody is pure hearted. As said in Puranas, in this era, everyone will ultimately perish thinking for themselves.’

Ved was thoughtless for a moment and had no argument to frame. ‘Even if humans are unpredictable and they lose their mind in greed. To such an unimaginable extent?’ he thought to himself.

‘Can we trust anyone?’ Ved asked sarcastically.

‘Let me help you with your thoughts, To be honest, I can’t say for sure, but I don’t think their inner selfishness or greed would have been the only factor that could be held responsible.’

Ved looked confused and waited for Yug to continue.

‘As I was telling you. The Manuscript, it has spiritual binding across it. There are things beyond what you know. Even in the twenty-first century, On this material side of the world, humans are yet to unleash their real potential.’ Yug replied.

Ved looked at the book. ‘It generated greed in them?’ He asked himself looking at it. If it were true, his mind couldn’t decide if the book was good or evil. He chose to ask the same. ‘So, Is this script cursed?’

‘It is. For the ones who wish to use it for their selfish motives.’ Yug calmly replied.

Ved was quick and asked in doubt. ‘Then why did you not let the one’s following you take it? It would have murdered them as well.’

Yug was silent. ‘In my vision, it was not the Granth that killed them.’ Yug continued. ‘I do not know the reason for the same. I saw only darkness and could hear terror. I could not see what murdered them.' Even Yug looked terrified as he remembered the roars. Yug skipped the topic and said. ‘Also, In this universe, there are forces with a lot more strength than you can imagine. If something is sealed and bound, the seal can be broken as well. To express in your language, They have similar mantras to decode it. Don’t underestimate their power.’

‘Oh Brahmin, but you seem very mighty, A simple jump of yours as you said, made a dent on a mountain.’ Ved said. ‘Can someone be stronger than you that you have to run from them?’

‘I was chased by a group, not an individual. Even if I am powerful, I couldn’t have sustained them all. And, it was not about me and my life. God forbid if they had got their hands on the Granth, the world would have turned upside down in even a week till now.’

‘But who are they?’ Ved asked.

‘You will get to know everything. Just wait for the right time.’ Yug said. ‘However, I am glad they did not get their hands on the manuscript.’

Ved was overwhelmed, hearing the power of the book and feared at the same time. ‘But how did I end up getting the Granth? You said the temple was not perceivable by eyes of even those mighty people following you. But I saw it, and that was where I got it. Why me?’

‘I have no answer to that. You have to come with me and find it out by yourself.’ Yug said.

‘Who will be answering my questions? Whom are we going to? Are we going to Ved Vyas?’ Ved asked desperately because he simply needed answers and wanted to find out if immortality actually existed.

‘As I have told you. Maharishi Vyas is not that simple to meet. We will be going to the head of my clan. He will probably answer all your questions.’ Yug replied.

Yug said probably while speaking and Ved noticed it. He understood that even Yug was unsure of some things. He did not want to stretch on the topic and push embarrassment on him. But his mind was disturbed. It did not seem to him that Yug was lying, but it was simply not possible for Ved to digest everything.

‘So, What I saw was future?’ Ved asked.

‘A possible future.’ Yug reanswered.

Ved tried to, but he couldn’t understand it. ‘But is it possible?’ He thought.

‘Can anyone, do it? Can anyone actually change the future?’ Ved asked reluctantly. ‘If I see future, I see it because it’s going to happen. But then it doesn’t happen. Meaning what I saw was not future?’

Hearing the paradox from Ved, Yug giggled a bit and said. ‘But what if these sequences of events were meant to happen? What you saw was not future, but a possibility, like a warning.’

‘Oh Brahmin, I see no answer to my question there? Can anyone change the future?’ Ved asked.

‘Yes. People say your future is in your hand. They do mean it. You shape what you become. The way you think, and act determines the future.’ Yug replied.

‘But if it goes by how you say, Then I changed future of 4 people including myself? My friends are not dead.’ Ved asked remembering his friends.

‘No, you changed the future of the whole world.’

Looking at Ved’s dazzled face, Yug continued ‘Each and every one of us, is linked. Every individual’s actions trigger actions of others in the world and vice versa.’ He smiled as he said.

Ved understood what Yug’s point. He again chose to be silent.

Yug decided to break the silence. ‘Do you mind giving me a glass of water?’

‘Yes sure.’ Ved rushed to the kitchen to get the same. Looking at the clean kitchen, he felt nostalgic. ‘Disha’ he remembered.

He took out his cell phone kept on silent mode to see two missed calls and a message from her asking him to call back. He thought of calling her, but that would rather be time-consuming he thought. He texted her back. ‘Will call you back in some time Disha. Sorry to keep you waiting…’

‘Ved?’ Yug called him, Ved rushed back to his room and handed him the glass.

Yug meanwhile looked around Ved’s things. He looked at all the material, the messy papers, his files and the old reference books. ‘These are your grandfather’s?’ asked Yug holding an old book in his hand.

Ved nodded.

‘He was an archaeologist too?’ Yug asked.

‘Yes. The best. And I wish to become like him.’ Ved replied.

‘Yes, he was. The very fact that he knew the verse shows his intensive research.’ Yug said remembering the verse Ved said his grandfather had told him. ‘The temple is very sacred, and its location is not mentioned anywhere. I wonder where he got to hear that. He had a pretty strong network I assume.’

Ved felt proud. He genuinely admired his grandfather.

Yug looked at the big messy graph on the wall drawn by Ved while researching on the Granth. ‘You are pretty intelligent. You found out a pretty good chunk of information with such limited resources.’

Yug gazed at Ved while analyzing the threads and their endpoints. ‘No wonder you were chosen.’ Yug smiled.

‘But how do you know I am the one?’ Ved was persistent. He wanted answers. With a mere I will come with you, Ved had put a pause to his ten years of professional career for something he did not have any clue about. At first, he had assumed the brahmin to be Ved Vyas, but he was disappointed later.

‘Because the first human who would get this manuscript was going to be the one.’ Yug said.

‘What does that mean? Even if you had placed it anywhere, any human would have taken it.’ Ved expressed his query.

‘No, it’s not that simple. There are lots of clauses to that as well. Also, you are capable as well. You are intelligent and fearless too.’ Yug replied.

Ved could understand Yug calling him intelligent as a compliment after viewing his research work, but he did not understand the other part. ‘Fearless?’ he asked.

Yug replied. ‘For an ordinary individual, someone mysteriously standing on the ninth floor on his balcony would have been a havoc. But, you reacted quite calmly. You are indeed fearless.’

Yug went speechless, and a pin drop silence spread across the room as he heard what Ved said. ‘Because… I knew you would come.’ Ved continued. ‘I was waiting for you.’
