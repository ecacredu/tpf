The Second Meet

Ved was stunned as he saw the mysterious figure slowly walking towards him. His calm voice claiming that the book belonged to him still echoed in Ved’s mind.

He couldn’t believe his eyes. The Brahmin looked simple, but his presence spilled power everywhere. Ved could feel the exceptional aura surrounding him. His dark green eyes did not let Ved look at them directly. His dressing was subtle but comfortable.

Being a literature expert, Ved knew 19 languages. But he couldn’t gather words in any of them, to say to him.

‘Everything did exist. It was never a myth.’ Ved thought thinking about Mahabharata. With sheer respect, he bent down and touched the sage’s feet.

‘Vijayi Bhava.' The Sage raised his palm and blessed him for success.

Keeping all his worries and depression aside, and gathering some courage to speak, joining his hands, asked in a mild tone. ‘Hey Great One, What is it that you seek?’

‘I wish for you to come with me. You have a purpose to serve.’ The sage replied.

With no hesitation, ‘I shall do whatever you say. I will come with you.’ Ved responded in an instant, but he had a lot of unanswered questions which he seeked answers for.

‘You look confused. What is troubling you?’ asked the Brahmin.

‘The situation.’ Ved said.

‘I would be pleased to hear what you have to say.’

Ved then told him the entire story, right from the beginning of his expedition to getting the Scripture. Then seeing his death, the death of his subordinates, and then them getting back to life.

Ved told him every part of what happened. However, the Sage seemed to emphasize on a particular part.

‘You saw the Shiva Temple?’ The sage asked his part of the question and continued. ’That is strange. It can't be seen by everyone. Also, how does your grandfather know about the Verse?’ He asked about the verse, which conveys description, about the Massive Shiva Temple which Ved saw.

‘I don’t know. I was in my mid age when grandfather lost his life. Never got a chance to ask such stuff at that time.’

‘What was your grandfather’s name? Also, state me your name.’

‘Bhaskar Trivedi, That’s my grandfather's name.’ As he spoke, his words reflected respect for his grandfather. ‘And, my name is Ved.’

‘Ved Trivedi it is.’ The great sage said addressing him. ‘What question do you have in your mind?’

‘TRRRINGG TRRRINGG … TRRRINGG TRRRINGG’

The interaction was broken all of sudden by the cell phone ring. It was Disha. He looked at the time. It was nine already. He had to leave, but some things are more important he thought and canceled the call.

He sent her a text message. ‘Disha, at a critical part of the research. Ttyl.’ He put the phone on silent and kept it aside.

‘Extremely Sorry for the noise.’ Ved continued. ‘Everything is changed in my life since I got this book. I was about to die. I was prepared to die. I closed my eyes for so. But I magically came back to our hotel room and saw the dreadful view.’

‘Who saved me? Why is all this happening to me?’ Ved pleaded seeking answers.

‘The manuscript did.’ The Brahmin said pointing at it.

Ved was even more confused now. He said, ‘Huh? How can a book save me?’

‘It is not a usual book. The manuscript has spiritual bindings on it.’

Ved had no shred of the doubt in what he said. It was complicated for him to think and he just chose to believe what he heard. Everything he had experienced came back to him. Also remembering an important part of this conversation, he asked. ‘Oh Great Sage, Can I ask you another question that bothers me?’

As the Sage nodded affirmatively, Ved continued ‘You said I have to go with you and that I have some purpose to serve. What is it?’

‘Some things are meant to be let for time to uncover. You will get to know soon.’ He replied.

‘But why me? I got the book accidently.’ Ved asked.

‘There is no such thing as an accident. The manuscript chose you. The fact that you saw the Shiva sculpture and the temple are in itself a huge thing. Though being so obvious, The marvelous sculpture can't be perceived by the eyes of an ordinary individual.’

‘The manuscript chose me?’

‘Yes. Or else you would not have got it in the first place. It was a matter of few minutes in which the book was collected by you, and your traces disappeared from near the temple, and I lost it.’

‘What do you mean by that, oh great sage?’

‘The fact is that I was there in the forest at that time, being chased by someone for this manuscript. I could have been killed, and the script would have been taken, but I reached the temple safely. And as I said, the temple is not perceptible to others. They lost the sight of me and so the book, which I then placed there to keep it safe for the time being and recollect later. But when I came back, the book was missing.’

‘You kept the book there? Do you mean… ’

‘Yes. The jolting sound you heard was me when I landed on the mountain. And someone running behind you might have been the one’s chasing me.’

Ved got the clarified that the sound was made by him, landing on the mountain. But while admiring, the sheer power of the Brahmin, Ved had an epiphany. ‘But… You were being chased? Who on earth can dare to do that? And how can you be killed Oh Great One? You are immortal, aren’t you?’

‘No?’

‘But as per ancient scripts, Ved Vyas, is supposedly immortal.'

‘Of course, he is. I wish to collect the other part of the manuscript to meet him. And to clear your possible confusion,’ The Brahmin continued. ’ I am not Maharishi Ved Vyas, but his follower. My Name is Yug.’
