CHAPTER

TWO

THE FACE OF DEATH

‘What? You found what?’ exclaimed Disha in disbelief.

He remained silent. He had carefully wrapped it with proper strings in a clean red cloth. He started unpacking the book. ‘I packed it and put it in when you knocked the door so that nobody finds out what I was up to.’

He unpacked it carefully. Disha was astonished. The book was large and looked ancient. It was almost made of bark, with a well-organized binding coated around. The book had traces of gold on it, whose shape resembled a hand. She could tell it even in candlelight; the gold was best of its kind.

‘What’s written here?’ she asked pointing her finger towards book title.

‘Granth Vyas’ Ved said impactfully.

She further opened the book and was amazed to see some pages had golden scripts on it. It was not practically possible. Its pages were literally made of thin finely sliced wood which gave it a real grungy feel.
