THE PERFECT AUTHOR

‘No it's not that Ved. You need rest, that’s it. And, sure, what do you want?’

‘I now know I am out of danger. I feel relieved. I will talk back to you at night. Can you leave me alone for some time?’ He insisted.

She knew Ved was sensible enough to understand things. She felt it is ok to let him have his space. ‘Ok. I understand your concern. I am staying in the hotel on the opposite road for today. We leave back tomorrow morning?’

‘Yes sure.’ He nodded.

She cleaned the place a bit to make it liveable. ‘Your grandfather was a pretty good archaeologist.’ She said looking at his awards in the hall showcase and went towards the door.

‘Your grandfather would have been proud of you today’ she said.

They both smiled at each other. Seeing her leave, Ved rushed back to the research room. Glazing at the book, he asked ‘What are you? What do you want from me?’

But now, his mind was free. He started his research work in full swing as he thought its the best way to divert his mind. Steve and others were fine, he was happy.
