FORTUNATE ENCOUNTER

THE PERFECT AUTHOR

The situation further tightened when he heard animal howls and roars. All the birds and animals in the forest showed their discomfort making peculiar sounds. Without further thoughts, deciding to follow his instinct, he jumped inside the temple.

Unlike the opening, it was huge inside. His torch showed him nothing but a straight dark walkable path which seemed to have no end. He preferred to run though. Contradicting to loud animal howls outside, it was chilling calm inside. He was already panting. The still dead air inside was making him suffocate. But then, He noticed a sharp reflection through his torch. Confused with the hope, he ran faster.

Ved finally reached there and was stunned to see that the light was nothing but his torch reflecting its light from a gold plated book half buried in the wall. It was loose as if just put in there.

The walls beside depicted mythological carvings. But, without wasting any time, Ved then kept the torch at a comfortable angle and started pulling it.

After few minutes of struggle, with his dirty hands, he finally managed to pull it out. The book was huge. It weighed more than a couple of kgs. He dusted and tried to clean it as it was covered in soil. Cleaned it enough to see its title in ancient Sanskrit.

‘Granth Vyas’ he read.
